The Muffin House

English muffin, first called a “toaster crumpet.” was invented in 1894 by a British immigrant to New York, Samuel Bath Thomas. Immediately embraced as a more elegant alternative to toast, it was served at fine hotels and ultimately became a mainstay of American breakfast cuisine.

337 West 20th Street is a four-story brick building that is officially known as “The Muffin House.” This is where Samuel Bath Thomas's invention happned. To this day, his oven is preserved in the basement of the building
