Romans and Focaccia

Its ancient origins are believed to have started around the 2nd century BC. The Phoenicians, Carthaginians, Greeks and tribes from the Levant baked the focaccia since then, using grains like millet, barkey or rye.

In Ancient Rome, panis focacius was a flat bread baked on the hearth. The word is derived from the focus and refers to cooking by a fireplace or hearth
