Cassava Processing by the Slaves in Brazil and Cheese Bread

Cheese bread has its roots in the culinary creations of African slaves.

The slaves, having mouths to feed, would soak and peel the cassava root, which was considered inedible by the landowners, and make bread rolls from it.

At the end of the 19th century, after slavery ended, other foods were made available to the Afro-Brazilian population. And in the State of Minas Gerais, the center of the dairy production in Brazil, cheese and milk were added to the balls of starch. So, with a combination of Afro-Brazilian ethnicity and the agricultural geography of Minas Gerais, cheese bread as we now all know it, came to be.
