Kipfel and Croissant

Croissant has its origin in Austria. The ancestor of croissant was called Kipfel, a crescent-shaped baked good featuring a generous amount of butter or lard and sometimes sugar and almonds.

According to popular lore, the kipfel originated in 1683 as a comestible celebration of Austrian victory over the Ottomans at the siege of Vienna.
