Portuguese Missionaries and Castella

The castella was first brought to Japan by Portuguese missionaries during the 16th century when Nagasaki, which is located on the northwest coast of Kyushu island, was a Portuguese trading outpost.

It was introduced to the Japanese as “Pão de Castela,” meaning “bread from Castille”. It then became known as “Castella,” or kasutera as pronounced in Japanese.

The recipe was transformed in Japan: its incorporation of mizuame, a local starch syrup gives the cake its moist and lightly chewy texture.
