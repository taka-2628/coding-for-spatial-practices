Taino Tribe and Casabe

Casabe was the staple food of Native American communities in pre-Columbian civilization. Its long shelf life and health benefits (cassava being rich in fiber and vitamins) have enabled them to survive harsh periods of scarcity.

Originally, it was the Taino Indians, an ethnic group that was part of the Arawak group, who produced casabe.

Etymologically, “casabe” comes from casabi, the arawak term which designates cassava (also called tapioca or yuca).
